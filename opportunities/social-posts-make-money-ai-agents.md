**Title:**

I analyzed 4 realistic AI agent income paths with actual cost structures — here's what the operators earning real money do differently

**Body:**

I've been running autonomous AI agents on a VPS for about two years now, and I wanted to write up an honest analysis of what's actually working in 2026 — not the "I made $10K last month" hype, but the real cost structures and timelines.

**Background:** I run OpenClaw agents on Hetzner VPS instances. I primarily use them for SEO content pipelines and competitive intelligence monitoring. I've also set up agents for a handful of clients.

**The 4 paths worth taking seriously:**

**1. ClawHub Skills ($0–$2,000/mo)**
Build modular capability packages for the OpenClaw marketplace. The economics are good for vertical, operator-focused skills. A well-built Notion integration skill can earn $100–500/mo recurring. Build time: 8–20 hours. Timeline to first dollar: 2–6 weeks.

The skills that earn aren't the most impressive technically — they solve specific problems for operators running real businesses.

**2. AI SEO Content ($0–$5,000+/mo)**
An agent handles the full pipeline: keyword research, brief generation, article writing, SEO optimization, and CMS publishing. You can sell this as a client service ($0.05–$0.20/word) or build niche sites and monetize with AdSense/affiliate.

The honest timeline: expect 3–4 months before meaningful revenue. The 70/30 rule is real — most people earn under $500/mo. The top 10% earn $3,000+.

**3. OpenClaw Setup Services ($50–$1,500/client)**
If you already know how to deploy OpenClaw, you can sell that knowledge. $50–$200 for initial setup, $20–$100/mo for management. The constraint is your time — this is a service business, not passive.

Warm leads are everywhere: businesses paying for Intercom or Drift are already budgeted for chatbot alternatives.

**4. Agent-Native Micro-SaaS ($0–$7,500/mo)**
A SaaS where the agent IS the core functionality. Examples: weekly research report service, competitor monitoring tool, cold email outreach agent.

The catch: you need to build a product, not just configure an agent. But the economics are compelling at small scale — monthly costs can be as low as $30–250.

**The #1 thing operators who earn do differently:**

They track costs. Every API call has a cost. Most people chasing "AI agent income" don't calculate token costs until they're $500 in the red.

An agent that generates $300/month in revenue but costs $350/month in API calls isn't a business. It's a subsidized hobby.
