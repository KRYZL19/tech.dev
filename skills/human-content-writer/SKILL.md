# human-content-writer

**name:** human-content-writer

**description:** Generate social media content that sounds like a real human wrote it. Use when: creating Twitter posts, LinkedIn posts, Reddit posts for TechStack.dev articles. NOT for: newsletter content, email content. Triggered by: social posts, twitter, linkedin, reddit, human-sounding content, not AI slop.

---

## Quick Reference

### What Makes Content Sound Human

**AI slop tells on itself:**
- Every paragraph perfectly structured
- Every bullet point gets equal length
- "Here's the thing..." or "In conclusion..." phrases
- Lists always numbered 1-2-3-4
- Ends every thought completely
- Never contradicts itself
- No rough edges

**Human content:**
- One point gets more energy than others
- Sometimes starts mid-thought ("so then I...")
- Has opinions that could be wrong
- Uses contractions naturally
- Varies sentence length wildly
- Says "I don't know" sometimes
- Has a specific voice/personality
- References specific things that happened
- Ends with a question or non-sequitur
- Casual punctuation: dashes, ellipsis..., "like" as a filler

## Platform Rules

### Twitter/X

- **1-2 tweets max, NOT threads of 5+**
- Hook is a hot take or specific observation — NOT a list预告
- No hashtag lists at the end — max 2 hashtags, integrated naturally
- Under 270 chars (leaves room for quote tweets)
- Good: "Spent $200 on API calls last month. Here's what I learned about heartbeat intervals the hard way."
- Bad: "5 tips for reducing AI agent costs! 🧵"

### LinkedIn

- First line stops the scroll with a hot take or surprising number
- Body is 3-4 paragraphs, NOT a bulleted list
- One section gets more detail than others
- Ends with a question or invitation to disagree
- No hashtags in the body text

### Reddit

- Title is "I tried X and [unexpected result]" format
- Body is conversational, starts in the middle of the story
- Includes what went wrong
- Less structured than LinkedIn — more stream of consciousness
- Link dropped naturally, not as an afterthought

---

## Usage

Given an article URL or text, follow the rules above to generate platform-appropriate content.

Input: Article URL or full article text
Output: Platform content for Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit
