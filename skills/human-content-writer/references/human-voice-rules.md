# Human Voice Rules

## Core Principle
The goal is content that sounds like a real person wrote it after thinking for a few minutes — not content generated to fill a content calendar.

## What AI Content Sounds Like

- Opens with "In today's digital age..." or similar generic framing
- Every paragraph is exactly 2-4 sentences
- Uses semicolons to connect thoughts (AI loves this)
- "Here's the thing..." to introduce points
- "It's important to note..." / "It's worth mentioning..."
- Concludes with a neat summary
- Bullet points are all roughly the same length
- Uses bold/emphasis on every other sentence
- Never says "I don't know" or expresses uncertainty
- Perfectly transitions between every topic

## What Human Content Sounds Like

- Opens mid-thought, or with a specific detail/opinion
- Sentence length varies from short to long within the same paragraph
- Uses contractions naturally (don't overdo it)
- Has rough edges: trailing thoughts, mid-sentence topic shifts
- Includes specific numbers or details that couldn't be faked
- Says what it actually thinks, even if controversial
- Sometimes ends abruptly, or with a question
- Uses dashes for asides — not for everything
- References real experiences or situations
- Less structured, more conversational
- Can include mild humor or self-deprecation

## Voice Checklist

Before publishing, ask:
- [ ] Does this sound like someone at a meetup said it?
- [ ] Would this person say "here's the thing" unironically?
- [ ] Is one section getting more energy than others?
- [ ] Does it end with a question or awkwardness, not a conclusion?
- [ ] Are there contractions or not?
- [ ] Is it under 270 chars (Twitter)?
- [ ] Any hashtags beyond 2 (Twitter)?

## Common Mistakes

1. **Lists are death** — Even if the source is list-based, convert to prose
2. **Hashtags** — AI adds 5-7 hashtags naturally. Humans add 1-2, awkwardly
3. **"It is important to note"** — Just say the thing
4. **Perfect transitions** — Real thoughts don't transition, they pivot
5. **Over-confidence** — Humans hedge. "I think", "might be", "not sure but"
6. **Generic hooks** — Specific > general every time
