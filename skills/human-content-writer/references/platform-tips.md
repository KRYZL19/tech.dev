# Platform-Specific Tips

## Twitter/X

### What Works
- Hot take opener: "Unpopular opinion:" or just a controversial statement
- Specific number + story: "Spent $X on..." / "Took me X hours to..."
- Callback to something specific
- Ending with a question that invites replies
- Mild self-deprecation
- One strong opinion per tweet

### What Doesn't Work
- Threads of 5+ tweets (nobody reads them)
- Numbered lists as hooks
- Thread-a-lists (thread of bullet points)
- "5 things you need to know about X!"
- Emoji everywhere
- More than 2 hashtags
- Hashtags in the middle of sentences that don't need them
- Parenthetical asides that are too long

### Character Limit
- 270 max (leaves room for quote tweets)
- If you can't say it in one tweet, condense or skip

### Example Good Hooks
- "Ran a $50/month VPS for 2 years. Never touched the firewall settings. Yesterday I found 47 failed SSH attempts from Brazil."
- "My AI agent spent $180 on API calls last month. Here's what I learned..."
- "The OWASP agentic Top 10 sounds made up but it's actually useful??"

### Example Bad Hooks
- "5 VPS Security Tips for AI Agents! 🧵👇"
- "Here's what you need to know about AI agent security:"
- "In today's digital landscape, securing your AI agents is more important than ever."

## LinkedIn

### What Works
- Surprising number or stat in first line
- Specific experience or case study
- Provocative statement about industry norm
- Personal failure story with lesson
- Question at the end that invites disagreement
- 3-4 paragraphs, first one short

### What Doesn't Work
- "I'm excited to share..." opener
- Bulleted lists in the body
- Hashtags in body (only OK in comments)
- Generic advice that could apply to any industry
- Overly polished corporate voice
- "Thoughts?" as ending (too soft, too expected)

### Length
- 3-4 paragraphs max
- First paragraph: 1-2 sentences that make you stop scrolling
- Last paragraph: a question, a challenge, or an admission

### Example Good Openers
- "Most AI agent security guides miss the point entirely."
- "I broke into my own VPS last week. On purpose. Here's what I found."
- "The OWASP agentic Top 10 exists now. It's as chaotic as it sounds."

## Reddit

### What Works
- "I tried X and [unexpected result]" title
- Conversational, slightly rambling tone
- Starting mid-story without full context setup
- Including failures, not just successes
- Genuine questions to the community
- Casual language (not corporate)
- Humor if it fits naturally

### What Doesn't Work
- Perfectly structured posts
- "Thought I'd share my experience" generic opener
- Direct links to own content as main post
- Promotional tone
- Being too positive without acknowledging tradeoffs
- Pretending to be objective about your own work

### Posting Etiquette
- Don't just drop a link — add context
- Engage with comments
- If posting own article, mention it openly: "I wrote about this and..."
- Don't format as a list

### Subreddits
- r/linuxadmin — sysadmin focused, appreciates technical depth
- r/homelab — hobbyist, less formal
- r/VPS — specific to VPS discussions
- r/selfhosted — for self-hosted services

## General Tips

1. **Write first, edit later** — First draft can be messy
2. **Read aloud** — If it sounds like a robot, rewrite
3. **Delete transitions** — "Additionally", "Furthermore", "In conclusion"
4. **Cut the filler** — "It's worth noting that", "The fact of the matter is"
5. **Add one rough edge** — A sentence that doesn't quite fit, a trailing thought
6. **One opinion** — Not a balanced view of all sides, pick one
