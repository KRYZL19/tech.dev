Best VPS for AI Agents and Bots in 2026

March 2026~12 min read

I went through way too many VPS providers trying to find the right one for running AI agents 24/7. Most guides just copy the same five providers with the same affiliate links. This is what I actually found after testing the ones that kept coming up.

What actually matters for AI agents

Skip the benchmarks you can't use. For running an autonomous agent, here's what actually matters:

  • RAM. If you're running anything that loads a model or keeps context windows open, you need more than 2GB. I started with 2GB and kept running into OOM kills.
  • Storage speed. NVMe makes a real difference when the agent is reading/writing logs, loading context, or handling files.
  • Uptime. The whole point is 24/7. If it goes down every other week, you've failed.
  • IPv6. More and more services require it. Some providers still don't offer it by default.
  • No noisy neighbors. Shared hosting where your neighbor's crypto miner tanks the CPU is a real thing.

What doesn't matter as much: max CPU single-core speed, unmetered bandwidth claims (you won't hit them), and most "enterprise" features.

The providers I tested

Hetzner

I've been using Hetzner for about six months now. They're a German company that doesn't mess around. The AX41-NVMe is the one most people recommend and I agree — 64GB RAM, NVMe storage, and it just works.

What I like: predictable pricing, no surprises on the bill, solid uptime in my experience. Their support actually answers, which is rarer than it should be.

What to watch: their IPv6 setup took me an extra 10 minutes to figure out. Not hard, just different from the standard Ubuntu defaults.

Contabo

Cheaper on paper. I used them for three months before switching. The specs look great for the price but I had two unexpected downtime incidents and their control panel is genuinely painful to use.

If price is the only factor and you don't mind the rough edges, Contabo works. But I'd pay the small premium for Hetzner instead.

Vultr

Good if you need global coverage — they have data centers everywhere. The Cloud Compute instances are solid. I used their Amsterdam location and it performed well.

The downside: their custom control panel is... a lot. Sometimes simple things take too many clicks.

DigitalOcean

The default choice for a reason. Great docs, predictable performance, easy to spin up and destroy instances. I've recommended them to people who are less technical because the interface is genuinely friendly.

More expensive than the European options. But if you want something that just works with minimal reading, it's hard to beat DigitalOcean.

Linode (Akamai)

Solid performer, nothing wrong with it. After the Akamai acquisition it's a bit unclear what the long-term roadmap looks like. Still reliable in my testing.

What I use and why

Hetzner. Not close. The AX41-NVMe gives me 64GB for roughly €45/month. I've had one unplanned reboot in six months. Their network has been consistently fast in Europe.

If I'm setting something up for a less technical person, I point them to DigitalOcean. The extra cost is worth the reduction in support emails.

Don't pay for what you don't need. If you're running one or two agents, a 16GB instance is probably fine. Save the 64GB for when you actually need it.

Forgetting to set up monitoring. UptimeRobot is free and tells you within 2 minutes when something goes down. Use it.

The bottom line

If you want the best mix of price, performance, and reliability: Hetzner AX41-NVMe. That's what I'm running.

If you want the easiest experience and don't mind paying a bit more: DigitalOcean.

If you're on a tight budget and can troubleshoot your own problems: Contabo works, just know what you're getting into.
